**Speaker:** Nathan Gotch (Rankability / Search OS)
**Research scope:** 10+ years of SEO campaign leadership across legal, health, B2B, and SaaS verticals; operator of two AI-driven SEO companies (Rankability and Search OS); author of *AI SEO for Dummies*

---

## Executive Summary

SEO in 2026 is no longer about ranking one URL in ten blue links. Nathan Gotch reframes it as **Search Everywhere Optimization** — a discipline that spans traditional search engines, multiple AI platforms, local search, e-commerce marketplaces, and social channels. The core insight: **traditional search is the fuel that powers AI platforms**, not a relic to abandon. Every AI assistant performing web retrieval (RAG) is pulling from the same indexes that Google and Bing power. Getting visible in traditional search remains non-negotiable — but it is now table stakes, not a strategy on its own.

Success in 2026 is measured across prompt coverage, citation share, brand mentions in AI answers, and platform presence — not SERP position alone.

## The Opportunity Map

### 1. Traditional Search

**Platforms:** Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo, Yahoo, Brave

Traditional search — the indexed web of pages and blue links — still drives traffic, but organic CTR is declining as AI features consume more SERP real estate. Its outsized importance in 2026 is as the **retrieval backbone for AI platforms**: when AI assistants perform web search (RAG), they draw heavily from traditional search indexes. Strong traditional rankings mean more AI citations.

**Why it still matters:**
- Direct traffic source (declining but still significant)
- Primary retrieval pool for AI web search (RAG)
- Platform authority that feeds AI answers indirectly

---

### 2. AI Search

AI search operates across two distinct mechanisms:

**Static Corpus (Training Data)**
When a model answers without triggering a web search, it draws from its training data. This has a cutoff date — anything published after that cutoff is invisible to the model's base knowledge. Influencing the static corpus requires building brand presence and citations *before* each training cycle ends, potentially 3–6 months before a model's public release.

**Web Search / RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)**
When a user asks a time-sensitive or specific question, AI platforms go to the web for up-to-date answers. The retrieved sources become citations in the AI's response. This is where active SEO influence happens in real time.

**Key AI platforms to track:**

| Platform | Notes |
|---|---|
| Google AI Overviews | Embedded in standard SERPs; dominates above-the-fold real estate |
| Google AI Mode | Separate interface; different citations than AI Overviews |
| Google Gemini | Longer, more contextual queries; distinct citation behavior |
| ChatGPT | 700M+ weekly active users; ~50% AI search market share |
| Claude (Anthropic) | Fast-growing; heavy use in productivity and co-work contexts |
| Perplexity | Popular AI-native search engine |
| Grok (xAI) | Heavily sourced from X (Twitter) content |
| Microsoft Copilot | Significant among enterprise and Microsoft ecosystem users |
| DeepSeek | Emerging; worth monitoring |

**Key insight:** These platforms largely copy each other's optimization signals. The execution is different per platform; the underlying strategy is consistent.

---

### 3. Local Search

Google dominates local queries. The **Google Business Profile (GBP)** is the center of local AI performance — performance in the local pack directly determines visibility in Google AI Mode for local-intent queries.

**How local AI answers are constructed:**
- Google AI Overviews for local queries pull from traditional web sources (Angie's List, Yelp, business websites)
- Google AI Mode for local queries pulls directly from **local pack rankings** — businesses that rank in the local pack appear in AI Mode answers

**Non-Google AI platforms** (ChatGPT, Perplexity, etc.) for local queries pull heavily from third-party directory platforms: Angie's List, Yelp, Better Business Bureau, and similar review aggregators. Getting listed and optimized on these platforms is the primary lever for local AI visibility outside Google.

---

### 4. E-Commerce Search

**Platforms:** Amazon, Walmart, Etsy, eBay

These are independent search ecosystems where standard SEO principles — relevance, completeness, review volume — apply in platform-specific ways. Businesses selling physical products must treat each platform's algorithm as its own optimization surface.

---

### 5. Social Search

**Platforms (by priority):** YouTube, LinkedIn, X (Twitter), Pinterest, TikTok, Instagram, Facebook

YouTube is the second most visited website globally and a primary retrieval source for AI Overviews and other AI platforms. Video transcripts are indexed and used by AI models to determine what a video is about — clarity of topic in-video matters as much as metadata.

**Topical authority on social platforms:** Algorithms reward channels that consistently produce content within a defined niche. Introducing off-topic content confuses discovery algorithms and dilutes performance — the same compounding focus principle that applies to websites applies to YouTube channels, LinkedIn profiles, and other social surfaces.

## Core Goals

### 1. Rank Your Own Website in Traditional Search
- Strong technical SEO: speed, mobile experience, security
- Deep topical coverage: create pages around every relevant sub-topic within your core expertise
- Backlinks: high-quality, relevant inbound links remain the strongest traditional ranking signal

### 2. Rank Other Properties Outside Your Website
Your domain is one citation source. AI platforms sample many. Expand visibility by ranking on high-authority platforms that themselves rank well in traditional search:

- **YouTube** — strong Google indexation; transcripts directly inform AI retrieval
- **LinkedIn Articles** — consistently indexed in traditional SERPs
- **Reddit and Quora** — heavily cited by AI platforms; community-driven authority
- **X (Twitter)** — growing indexation in traditional search
- **Instagram and Facebook** — emerging indexation for branded and topical queries

**Strategy:** Rather than building one winning page, attack core topics across every platform known to rank in traditional search. Each ranking presence becomes an additional citation path into AI answers.

### 3. Get Your Brand Mentioned in AI Answers for Commercial-Intent Queries
Target queries like *"what are the best [category] tools for [use case]"* and verify whether your brand appears in the AI answer across each platform. Gaps in AI visibility represent actionable citation opportunities.

### 4. Become a Top Retrieval Source
Your domain should appear as a frequently cited source when AI platforms retrieve results for your target topics. Monitor citation share — not just rankings.

### 5. Be Present on the Top Retrieval Sources
Identify which third-party sites AI platforms repeatedly cite for your category and build brand presence there. An **unlinked brand mention** on a high-authority retrieval source still influences AI answers.

## Execution

### Tracking and Gap Analysis

You cannot optimize what you do not measure. Build a prompt library across the full buyer journey:

- **Problem queries** — *how do I [solve specific problem]*
- **Category queries** — *best [product/service category]*
- **Comparison queries** — *[Brand A] vs [Brand B]*
- **Alternatives queries** — *best alternatives to [competitor]*
- **Integration queries** — *[product] for [use case or team type]*

For each AI platform, identify:
- Is your brand cited?
- Which sources are cited most frequently?
- Where is your brand completely absent?

Use citation data to prioritize outreach — find the gaps, then go earn presence on those retrieval sources.

### AI-Assisted Execution

**Beginner level:** Use ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude for research, content drafts, strategic questions, and data analysis. Fast but limited to task-level assistance without continuity.

**Advanced level — Agents:** AI agents (Claude Co-work, Open Claude / OpenClaw, or similar) can be directed to execute tasks autonomously: link prospecting, profile audits, platform updates, content research. Key considerations:

- **Treat agents like entry-level employees**: they require ongoing iteration and skill-building to produce reliable output
- **Provide precise prompts**: vague instructions produce vague output; invest in prompt quality
- **Save outputs immediately**: browser-based agent sessions can crash and lose work; copy results to a permanent document before closing
- **Agents execute strategy — they do not create it**: process knowledge and SEO fundamentals are still required to direct agents effectively

## Actionable Checklist

### Measurement
- [ ] Define 2–3 prompts per buyer-journey stage for your business
- [ ] Track **brand visibility** — % of prompts where your brand appears, per platform
- [ ] Track **citation share** — % of citations pointing to your domain
- [ ] Track **accuracy** — % of AI answers that correctly describe your product or service
- [ ] Track performance separately across Google AI Overviews, AI Mode, Gemini, ChatGPT, and Perplexity (they do not produce identical results)

### Traditional Search
- [ ] Audit technical SEO: Core Web Vitals, mobile experience, HTTPS
- [ ] Map and close content gaps across your core topical area
- [ ] Build backlinks from relevant, high-authority domains

### AI and Citation Strategy
- [ ] Identify top citation sources per AI platform for your target queries
- [ ] Verify brand presence on those sources; build profiles or pursue mentions where absent
- [ ] Prioritize unlinked brand mentions on high-retrieval domains — they influence AI answers even without a link

### Content and Distribution
- [ ] Publish video content on YouTube for high-intent topics
- [ ] Write LinkedIn Articles targeting commercial and comparison queries
- [ ] Participate authentically in Reddit discussions that feed AI retrieval
- [ ] Build out comparison, alternatives, and product pages (bottom-of-funnel content most cited by AI)
- [ ] Ensure key pages use clean structure, clear headings, and excerptable copy

### Local (if applicable)
- [ ] Optimize and maintain your Google Business Profile
- [ ] Pursue rankings in the local pack — this directly drives Google AI Mode visibility
- [ ] Build presence on Yelp, Angie's List, BBB, and other directory platforms cited by non-Google AI for local queries

### Weekly Workflow
- [ ] Review AI visibility metrics weekly
- [ ] Ship at least 2–3 fixes per week (new content, citation outreach, platform presence)
- [ ] Log changes in prompt coverage and citation share over time

## Honest Caveats

**Traditional SEO is not dead.** Organic CTR is declining, but traditional search indexes are the primary retrieval source for AI platforms. Abandoning traditional SEO undermines AI visibility at the same time.

**There is no shortcut.** AI tools and agents dramatically accelerate execution but cannot substitute for process knowledge. An agent given to someone without SEO experience produces nothing useful. The strategy must come first.

**AI platforms are non-deterministic.** The same query produces different answers and cites different sources across platforms, models, and even repeated runs. Track performance systematically — do not rely on spot checks.

**The opportunity is growing.** The discovery surface is larger than it has ever been. Brands that understand Search Everywhere Optimization and execute consistently across traditional search, AI platforms, and third-party properties will capture disproportionate visibility as the landscape matures.
