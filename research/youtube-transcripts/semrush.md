## Executive Summary

Semrush has embedded AI across its entire platform — from the moment you log in to the moment you publish. Rather than offering a single AI writing tool, the product suite uses AI to surface prioritized recommendations, personalize keyword difficulty scores, predict traffic potential, track Google AI Overviews, optimize content against live SEO data, and generate a full month of social media content from a single domain input.

The throughline across every tool is **data-informed prioritization**: AI highlights what to work on first, estimates the impact of each action relative to your specific domain, and guides execution through the same platform. The result is a workflow that compresses research, strategy, creation, and publishing into a single connected loop.

## Feature Breakdown

### 1. Semrush Copilot — AI Dashboard Assistant

Copilot is the first thing you see on login. It monitors your active Semrush projects and surfaces the highest-priority issues and opportunities, ranked by impact.

**What Copilot flags:**
- Lost or gained authority backlinks
- Keyword ranking drops for tracked terms
- Competitor ranking movements
- Low-hanging-fruit keywords within reach

Beyond flagging issues, Copilot links directly to the relevant tool for each recommendation and can walk you through initial setup. It also helps prioritize across multiple projects when you are managing more than one domain — surfacing what matters most across all of them in a single view.

---

### 2. Keyword Overview — Personalized Keyword Difficulty

Standard keyword difficulty scores apply to the web at large. Semrush's AI personalizes this metric to your specific domain.

**How it works:**
Enter a keyword and your domain. The AI algorithm evaluates:
- Relevance between your domain and the keyword
- Competitor domain metrics for that keyword
- Competition density within the topic
- Your domain's topical authority for the keyword cluster

The output is a **personal keyword difficulty score** — showing not just how hard a keyword is generically, but how hard it is *for you specifically*, relative to the domains already ranking for it. In some cases your personal score will be higher than the generic score; in others, lower. This changes prioritization decisions meaningfully.

**Potential traffic predictions:** The tool also estimates how much traffic your site could receive if you ranked for the keyword and its related topic cluster, based on your personal difficulty score and competitor benchmarks. This makes the value of each keyword tangible before you invest in content.

---

### 3. Keyword Magic Tool — Topic Expansion with Personal Metrics

From Keyword Overview, you can jump directly to Keyword Magic Tool to expand any topic into a full set of related keywords and questions — all with the same personalized difficulty and traffic prediction metrics applied.

Select any keywords from the results and send them directly to Keyword Strategy Builder.

---

### 4. Keyword Strategy Builder — Keyword List to Content Map

Keyword Strategy Builder takes a list of keywords and transforms it into a structured content strategy.

**What it produces:**
- Keywords grouped into topic clusters
- Each cluster mapped to a supporting article or page
- All cluster content linked to a central **pillar page** — the primary content piece for the topic

The pillar-and-cluster architecture serves two purposes: it signals comprehensive topical coverage to search engines, making crawling and indexing more efficient, and it helps users understand the breadth of your content on a given subject.

Clicking into any topic cluster gives a detailed breakdown of keywords, their metrics, and the recommended content structure. The output is a ready-to-implement content plan generated in minutes.

---

### 5. Position Tracking — AI-Powered Rank Monitoring and AI Overview Detection

Position Tracking monitors your rankings and calculates AI-based estimates of traffic potential, visibility, and average position across your tracked keywords.

**AI Overview tracking:** Semrush has built specific tooling to track Google's AI Overview feature across four levels of granularity:

- **Sensor** — Industry-wide view of how frequently AI Overviews appear across different sectors; useful for assessing how much your category is affected
- **Domain Overview → Overview Tab** — Shows what percentage of a domain's total ranking keywords appear in AI Overviews
- **Organic Research → SERP Features Table** — Lists specific keywords where a domain appears in AI Overviews, split by whether the domain is linked or unlinked within the Overview
- **Position Tracking → AI Overview Filter** — Tracks AI Overview appearances specifically for the keywords in your active campaign, showing whether your site is linked in each instance

This four-level view gives you both the macro picture (how prevalent are AI Overviews in your industry?) and the micro detail (which of my specific keywords trigger an AI Overview, and am I in it?).

### 6. SEO Writing Assistant — Real-Time Content Optimization

SEO Writing Assistant scores content across four dimensions as you write or after you paste existing text:

- **SEO** — Coverage of semantically related keywords found in top-performing articles for the target keyword
- **Readability** — Sentence and paragraph complexity relative to your target audience
- **Originality** — Plagiarism detection to flag potential duplication issues
- **Tone of voice** — Consistency of tone across the piece

**How the SEO scoring works:** The tool identifies related keywords present in top-ranking articles for your target term and flags which ones are missing from your content. Adding them increases your SEO score and makes the content more comprehensive relative to what is already ranking.

**AI editing features within the tool:**
- Split or simplify paragraphs on suggestion
- Rephrase individual sentences
- Generate entirely new paragraphs on request when existing content needs replacement

The tool is available as a standalone editor within Semrush, as a Google Docs add-on, and as a WordPress plugin — allowing you to optimize within whichever environment you already work in.

### 7. Social Content AI — Full-Cycle Social Media Content Generation

Social Content AI generates a complete social media content plan from your domain URL and a brief business description. It supports Facebook, Instagram, X (Twitter), LinkedIn, Google Business Profile, and Pinterest.

**Workflow:**

1. **Input:** Enter your domain and a short description of your business, current goals, or upcoming campaigns (e.g. an upcoming webinar). The description steers content priorities without requiring you to update your website first.

2. **Idea generation:** The tool surfaces two types of content ideas:
   - **Trending ideas** — based on current news and developments in your industry
   - **Evergreen ideas** — organized by topic pillars relevant to your domain

   A single generation produces approximately 30 post ideas — enough for a full month of content.

3. **Post drafting:** Select any idea and choose your social network, tone of voice, and post type. The tool drafts the post with platform-appropriate formatting including hashtags and emoji. From the draft you can regenerate, rephrase, shorten, or expand.

4. **Visual generation:** Generate illustrations, photos, or videos to accompany the post using built-in design templates. The aspect ratio auto-suggests the correct format for the selected platform. Generated videos can be used as-is or customized in the built-in editor (video, captions, fonts, and design).

5. **Publishing:** Send directly to Semrush Poster for scheduling, or save the draft for later.

**Alternative input modes:**
- **From scratch** — Enter your own ideas and let AI write the post
- **Link-based** — Paste a blog URL and select a post format; the tool writes platform-native content from the article

**Brand consistency:** Social Content AI retains the brand identity of your domain across all generated posts, ensuring consistency in tone and style without manual configuration per post.

## Actionable Checklist

### Initial Setup
- [ ] Log in to Semrush and review Copilot recommendations — address backlink losses and keyword drops before expanding into new content
- [ ] Add your domain to Keyword Overview and generate a personal keyword difficulty baseline for your top 10–20 target keywords
- [ ] Set up Position Tracking for your primary keyword campaign; enable the AI Overview filter

### Keyword Research
- [ ] Run personal keyword difficulty scores for any keyword before committing to content creation
- [ ] Use potential traffic predictions to prioritize keywords with the best estimated return relative to your domain's current authority
- [ ] Export high-potential keywords from Keyword Magic Tool to Keyword Strategy Builder
- [ ] Review the pillar-and-cluster map produced by Keyword Strategy Builder; validate it against your existing content before publishing new pages

### AI Overview Monitoring
- [ ] Check Sensor to understand how frequently AI Overviews appear in your industry
- [ ] Review Domain Overview to see what share of your ranking keywords currently appear in AI Overviews
- [ ] Use Organic Research to identify which specific keywords trigger AI Overviews and whether your domain is linked or unlinked
- [ ] Monitor AI Overview appearances weekly via Position Tracking for your tracked keyword set

### Content Creation
- [ ] Run every article through SEO Writing Assistant before publishing — target green scores across SEO, readability, and originality
- [ ] Add missing related keywords flagged by the tool to improve topical comprehensiveness
- [ ] Use the paragraph generation feature for sections that need rewriting rather than editing line by line
- [ ] Run the originality check on any content that has been heavily AI-assisted or repurposed

### Social Media
- [ ] Set up Social Content AI with your domain and a description that includes any near-term campaigns or announcements
- [ ] Generate a monthly content plan (30 ideas) in one session; sort by evergreen and trending buckets
- [ ] Draft, generate visuals, and schedule posts in a single workflow using Social Content AI → Poster
- [ ] Use the link-based input to repurpose existing blog content into platform-native social posts

## Honest Caveats

**Personalized metrics require an active project.** The personal keyword difficulty score and traffic predictions are most accurate when Semrush has sufficient data from your domain's history and competitor landscape. New or low-traffic domains will see less precise estimates initially.

**AI Overview tracking is still evolving.** Google's AI Overviews are relatively new and their behavior — which queries trigger them, which sites are cited — continues to shift. Whether AI Overview appearances help or hurt traffic remains an open question; the Semrush tooling helps you monitor, but the strategic response is still being worked out industry-wide.

**SEO Writing Assistant scores content, not ranking outcomes.** A high score improves the likelihood of ranking but does not guarantee it. Treat the score as a quality floor, not a ranking prediction.

**Social Content AI is a starting point.** Generated posts reflect your domain's public content and the brief description you provide. Brand voice nuance, proprietary positioning, and campaign-specific messaging still require human review and editing before publishing.
