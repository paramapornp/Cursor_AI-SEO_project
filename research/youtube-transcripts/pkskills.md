## Executive Summary

Most AI writing tools produce content that needs significant manual work before it is SEO-ready — particularly around internal linking, which platforms typically skip entirely. This workflow solves that by feeding Claude your website's sitemap first, giving it the context needed to generate internal links automatically as part of the written content.

The result is a service page or blog post that comes out of Claude with correct heading hierarchy, relevant backlinks, and internal links already embedded — ready to paste into WordPress or Elementor with minimal editing. Pages written this way have ranked at the top of Google for competitive local service keywords, with green SEO and readability scores in SEO auditing tools.

---

## Why This Approach Works

### Sitemap as Context

Standard AI prompts produce generic content because the model has no knowledge of your website's structure. By passing your sitemap URL to Claude first, you give it a complete map of your existing pages. Claude can then reference those pages naturally within the written content as internal links — something almost no other AI-generated content workflow achieves automatically.

### Structured Prompting in Two Stages

The workflow uses two sequential prompts rather than one. The first prompt establishes Claude's understanding of your business: your services, locations, contact details, and content strategy. The second prompt requests the actual page. This separation means Claude writes with full business context, producing content that reflects your specific company rather than a generic version of it.

## Step-by-Step Workflow

### Step 1: Locate Your Sitemap

Find your website's sitemap. For most WordPress sites it is accessible at:

```
yourdomain.com/sitemap.xml
```

or

```
yourdomain.com/page-sitemap.xml
```

The sitemap lists all indexed URLs on your site. Copy the full sitemap URL — you will paste this into Claude in the next step.

### Step 2: Send the Business Context Prompt to Claude

Open Claude and paste your sitemap URL along with the first prompt. This prompt is designed to give Claude a complete picture of your business before any writing begins. It should include:

- **Company name**
- **Phone number and email**
- **Physical address**
- **Services offered**
- **Target locations or service areas**
- **Any other details specific to your business** (e.g. industry specialization, certifications, unique selling points)

The full prompt template is available in the video description. Customize it with your own business details before submitting.

After submitting this prompt, Claude will confirm its understanding of your website structure, service areas, and business context. It is now primed to write content that reflects your actual company.

### Step 3: Send the Page Template Prompt

Submit the second prompt, which instructs Claude to prepare the structural template for the page — heading hierarchy, section layout, and content approach. This prompt is also available in the video description.

This step bridges the business context Claude now has with the format requirements of a well-structured SEO page.

### Step 4: Enter Your Target Keyword

Provide Claude with the specific keyword you want the page to rank for. For example:

> *"Industrial and mining calibration services in Australia"*

Claude will write the complete page using this keyword as the focus, incorporating:

- **H1** — primary keyword-focused page title
- **H2 headings** — section structure covering the topic comprehensively
- **Internal links** — referencing relevant pages from your sitemap
- **Backlinks** — outbound references to relevant authoritative sources
- **Body copy** — SEO-optimized, readable content built around your keyword and business details

Review the output before publishing. Because Claude was given your actual business information in Step 2, the content will be specific to your company rather than generic.

### Step 5: Publish to WordPress

Copy the generated content and paste it into your WordPress editor (Gutenberg, Elementor, or whichever page builder you use), mapping the heading structure correctly:

- Claude's H1 → your page's H1 title
- Claude's H2s → section headings in your editor
- Body text → pasted into the corresponding content blocks

Style and design the page to match your site. Publish when ready.

## Honest Caveats

**This workflow is most effective for local and niche service keywords.** The demonstrated results — top rankings for calibration services in Western Australia — are in a specific, relatively low-competition local market. Results in highly competitive national or global niches will vary significantly.

**Sitemap-based internal linking requires a sitemap with real, indexed pages.** New websites with few published pages will see limited internal linking benefit because Claude has fewer URLs to reference. The approach compounds in value as your site grows.

**Manual review is still required.** Claude writes from the business context you provide, but factual accuracy, tone consistency, and link relevance should be checked before publishing. Internal links generated from the sitemap should be verified to point to the correct pages.

**Prompt quality determines output quality.** The two-prompt structure only works well if the business context prompt is filled in thoroughly. Sparse or vague business details produce generic content. Invest time in the prompt templates before scaling the workflow across multiple pages.

**AI-generated content quality signals matter.** Pages passing SEO and readability audits (green scores) perform better than those that do not. Run every published page through an SEO audit tool and address any flagged issues before considering the page complete.
