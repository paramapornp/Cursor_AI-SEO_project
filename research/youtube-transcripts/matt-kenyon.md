# Mastering AI Search in 2026: 7-Step Plan

**Source:** [Surfer Academy YouTube — AI Search Strategy](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8YRvsoY16Wc)  
**Speaker:** Matt Kenyon (Kenyon Digital)  
**Research scope:** Surfer onboarding survey (25% of new customers from AI search), 36 million AI Overviews analyzed, Google impression/CTR shifts post-AI Overviews, ChatGPT 1B+ weekly searches, 8 years of Surfer SEO investment (based on Surfer CMO Tom Naga's article)

---

## Executive Summary

Matt Kenyon breaks down Surfer's real-world AI search results—not theory, but revenue flowing from customers who discover brands via ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. The core finding: **AI SEO is traditional SEO extended for assistant-driven discovery**, layered on top of a strong existing foundation. There is no secret tactic or single line of code; Surfer's dominance comes from **8 years of consistent investment in SEO, content, and user experience**.

Organic clicks are no longer the sole success metric—impressions rose ~49% while CTR fell ~30% after AI Overviews—but **bottom-of-funnel brand queries are increasingly answered inside AI interfaces**, which can work in your favor. Success is measured through prompt coverage, citation share, assistant recall, and platform presence—not just SERP positions.

---

## Key Strategies

### 1. Track What Matters (Baseline Visibility)

You cannot manage what you do not measure. Surfer started by adding ~100 prompts to their AI tracker across the full buyer journey.

**Prompt categories to track:**
- **Problem queries** — e.g., *how to improve SEO rankings*
- **Category queries** — e.g., *best AI SEO tools*
- **Comparison queries** — e.g., *Surfer vs Semrush*
- **Alternatives queries** — e.g., *best alternatives to Ahrefs*
- **Integration queries** — e.g., *SEO tools for content teams*

**Recommendation:** Define at least **2–3 prompts per category** for your own business.

**Tracker quality matters:** Weak tools scrape one answer once daily. Strong tools run prompts multiple times, test across models, expose cited sources, and recommend concrete actions.

---

### 2. Earn Missing Mentions

AI assistants heavily cite **third-party sources**—Reddit, LinkedIn, editorial sites, and "best of" roundup posts—not vendor homepages.

For queries like *best accounting software* or *best email marketing platform*, roundup lists often dominate citations. If competitors appear and you do not, outreach is required.

**Outreach playbook:**
- Email publishers of roundup posts and ask how to get listed.
- Sometimes one or two emails suffice; other times it requires affiliate deals or small sponsorship fees.
- Paid placements can compound for years when they improve AI search visibility—the ROI often justifies the cost.

---

### 3. Create Content That Replaces Competitor Citations

If assistants keep citing a competitor's page, the fix is straightforward: **write the better page**.

**Surfer case study:** They optimized a versus article for entity coverage, added counterarguments, and pushed it to #1. Within weeks, assistants switched citations from the competitor to Surfer.

**Distribution tip:** Publish on high-authority surfaces where LLMs look first—**LinkedIn articles** or appropriate **Reddit posts**—not only on your own domain.

**Key ingredients:** Factual clarity and entity coverage—the same elements assistants rely on when selecting sources.

---

### 4. Publish Bottom-of-Funnel Content First

AI assistants lean heavily on **middle- and bottom-funnel "money pages"**, especially for branded terms—while top-of-funnel informational queries are increasingly absorbed by AI Overviews.

**Money pages include:**
- Product and solutions pages
- Comparison pages (you vs. competitors)
- Alternatives pages (competitors vs. your product)—a powerful way to control narrative on your own site

**Buyer behavior shift:** A user may ask ChatGPT *best email software*, then follow up with *compare these three platforms*. If you lack comparison, review, or roundup pages, **competitors shape the short list instead of you**.

**Content approach:** Prioritize BOFU templates—product roundups, comparisons, product descriptions—with custom brand knowledge, voice/tone settings, and competitor references baked in.

---

### 5. Show Up Where AI Assistants Are Looking

Surfer's analysis of **36 million AI Overviews** found assistants repeatedly pull from **YouTube videos, Reddit threads, and LinkedIn articles**.

These are no longer side channels—they **directly shape AI answers**.

**Actions:**
- Publish video content
- Join and contribute to relevant Reddit discussions
- Seed LinkedIn posts that restate your point of view and expertise

---

### 6. Optimize for AI Extraction, Not Just Ranking

This step is fundamentally **good classic SEO**, but worth repeating explicitly: optimize so content can be easily extracted and cited.

**What works:**
- Clean HTML and clear hierarchical headings
- Lists and tables where appropriate
- **Context-inclusive language**—copy that can be lifted and excerpted in an AI Overview or citation
- Short Q&A blocks

Assistants do not just rank pages—they **chunk and excerpt** the most useful segments. Structure content so the best passages are easy to find and reuse.

---

### 7. Measure, Learn, and Repeat

AI search optimization is **not a one-off campaign**—it must be baked into weekly marketing workflows. Consistency compounds into a moat.

**Weekly rhythm (3 actions):**
1. Check your AI tracker KPIs—is brand visibility rising or falling?
2. Ship **2–3 fixes** (new versus page, roundup outreach, YouTube video, etc.)
3. Track changes in prompt coverage, citation share, and accuracy

## Actionable Insights

### Measurement & KPIs
- [ ] Define 2–3 prompts per buyer-journey category (problem, category, comparison, alternatives, integration).
- [ ] Track **LLM share of voice** — % of prompts where your brand appears.
- [ ] Track **citation share** — % of citations pointing to your domain.
- [ ] Track **accuracy score** — % of AI answers that state your product facts correctly.
- [ ] Track **platform coverage** — presence on YouTube, Reddit, LinkedIn, and high-authority listicles assistants cite.

### Mentions & Outreach
- [ ] Identify roundup posts where competitors are listed but you are not.
- [ ] Outreach to editors for inclusion (email, affiliate, or sponsorship as needed).
- [ ] Treat paid roundup placements as long-term AI visibility investments when ROI supports it.

### Content Strategy
- [ ] Build and prioritize BOFU pages: comparisons, alternatives, product/solutions pages.
- [ ] Write superior versus/alternatives content to displace competitor citations.
- [ ] Optimize for entity coverage, factual clarity, and counterarguments on key money pages.
- [ ] Shift focus from pure top-of-funnel volume toward pages buyers and assistants use for short-list decisions.

### Distribution Channels
- [ ] Publish YouTube videos on topics your buyers ask AI assistants about.
- [ ] Participate authentically in Reddit threads that feed AI answers.
- [ ] Publish LinkedIn articles restating your POV on high-intent topics.
- [ ] Republish or syndicate key content on high-authority third-party surfaces where appropriate.

### On-Page & Extraction
- [ ] Audit key pages for clean HTML, heading hierarchy, lists, tables, and Q&A blocks.
- [ ] Write context-inclusive copy that stands alone when excerpted.
- [ ] Ensure money pages are structured for both traditional ranking and AI citation.

### Weekly Workflow
- [ ] Review AI tracker KPIs every week.
- [ ] Ship at least 2–3 visibility fixes per week (content, outreach, or distribution).
- [ ] Log changes in prompt coverage, citation share, and accuracy over time.

## Honest Caveat

**SEO and Google are not dead.** Google still holds ~90% of global search share, and AI is simultaneously changing how people search—both can be true. ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude largely use **similar ranking signals to Google**, with possible extra weight on off-page mentions, drawing from the same indexes (Google, Bing, YouTube).

There is **no secret GEO tactic**. Surfer's AI search success reflects years of foundational SEO—not a hack. **AI SEO (or GEO) is a skill set layered on strong SEO**, not a separate discipline you can skip straight to.

The discovery pie is still growing; it is just **sliced differently**. Top-of-funnel clicks may shrink, but brand-specific bottom-funnel queries inside AI assistants can surface your business for free. Treat the seven-step plan as an ongoing process—measure, fix, repeat—not a single campaign.
